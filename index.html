What is COVID-19?

Coronaviruses are large group of viruses that cause illness in humans and animals. Rarely, animal coronaviruses can evolve and infect people and then spread between people such as has been seen with MERS and SARS.

Origin

COVID-19 is the infectious disease caused by the most recently discovered coronavirus. This new virus and disease were unknown before the outbreak began in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. COVID-19 is now a pandemic affecting many countries globally. It is a double-edged sword as it is killing both heath and economy of the world.

Spread

People can catch COVID-19 from others who have the virus. The disease spreads primarily from person to person through small droplets from the nose or mouth, which are expelled when a person with COVID-19 coughs, sneezes, or speaks. These droplets are relatively heavy, do not travel far and quickly sink to the ground. People can become infected by touching these objects or surfaces, then touching their eyes, nose or mouth. This virus is likely to stay for a long time on surfaces hence spreading quickly.

Success Stories

The pandemic has undoubtedly changed our lives. While some people cope better than the others, it's necessary to keep our hopes up no matter what how hard the situation.

Take a minute to look at some civilians who succesfully have fought and recovered from Covid-19 and shared their experience.

"I am grateful to Allah for giving me a new lease of life.I was under severe mental stress after getting to know about the COVID-19 status. However, the people around me like doctors and nurses all along told me that I will recover and they proved right"

Abdhul Rehman, resident of Pipili area in Puri district, who is also a Nizamuddin returnee

"I was really scared when I first saw the results.However, thanks to the Almighty's grace and the good treatment given by the doctors I recovered slowly. It was a tough journey but I'm glad my body could fight for me"

Gopichand Mehra, a 67 year old residing in Bangalore

"I was carrying when I tested positive and was more worried about the health of the unborn. But by God's grace my son was born healthy . And thank god for the timely treatment I recovered and returned home safely. Although it was a really rough month , I'm glad we both are safe "

Reema Sharma, resident of Mumbai
